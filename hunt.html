Hunting grounds

Mt. Hood - Oregon

According to bigfootmap.com this is the single highest concentration of sightings in a condensed area. The chance of an encounter is therefore the highest.

Skookum Meadows - Washington

This area is known for its dense forests and rugged terrain, making it an ideal habitat for Bigfoot. Numerous sightings and encounters have been reported here over the years.

Blue Mountains - Oregon

The Blue Mountains are a remote and rugged area with dense forests and deep canyons. This region has been the site of numerous Bigfoot sightings and encounters, making it a popular destination for hunters.

Redwood National and State Parks - California

The towering redwoods provide ample cover for Bigfoot, and the parks have a history of sightings. The dense forests and remote areas make it a prime location for those seeking to encounter the elusive creature.

The Adirondack Mountains - State of New York

The Adirondacks are known for their vast wilderness areas and dense forests. This region has been the site of numerous Bigfoot sightings, particularly in the more remote areas.

Great Smoky Mountains - Tennessee/North Carolina

The Great Smoky Mountains are a popular destination for hikers and outdoor enthusiasts. The dense forests and rugged terrain provide an ideal habitat for Bigfoot, and there have been numerous sightings reported in this area.

Overall Prepwork

Your Final Steps

Dress in weather-appropriate, quiet clothing and pack enough food, water, and first aid supplies for your trip. Learn how to identify animal tracks and sounds, and bring notebooks or recording devices to document evidence. Finally, plan your route, inform someone of your whereabouts, and always prioritize safety while exploring the wilderness—after all, the hunt for Bigfoot is as much about the adventure as the discovery itself.

Remember to respect wildlife and the environment during your expedition.
